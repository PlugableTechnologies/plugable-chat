A rich mix of public information, press releases, and media coverage leading into the show has now materialized into live keynotes, exhibits, on-site experiences, and curated tours.

This report covers:

* Event overview, keynotes, and Media Days
* On-site experiences and curated tours
* Major themes and categories

  * Automotive and mobility
  * Consumer electronics and smart home
  * Artificial intelligence
  * Robotics and drones
  * Digital health and wellness
  * Sustainability and green tech
  * Gaming, XR, and entertainment
  * Emerging tech and future trends

All descriptions preserve the full detail available as of late 2025, written for readers attending CES 2026.

## 3. Automotive and Mobility

Vehicle technology is one of the defining pillars of CES 2026, with emphasis on electric vehicles, autonomous driving, humanoid and industrial robots, and new mobility concepts.

### 3.1 Sony Honda Mobility (AFEELA)

* Major presence with its first independent CES press conference on January 5 (Media Day)
* Exhibiting:

  * AFEELA 1 pre production electric sedan
  * An all new concept car
* At LVCC Central Hall: multiple AFEELA 1 vehicles in different colors
* Target: 2026 California launch timeline
* Brand pillars:

  * Autonomy
  * Augmentation
  * Affinity
* Focus: advanced sensors, infotainment, and human centric design that merges consumer electronics know how with automaking

### 3.2 Hyundai Motor Group and Robotics

Hyundai uses CES 2026 to present a broad vision that spans vehicles, robotics, and factory automation.

* Atlas humanoid robot by Boston Dynamics is publicly unveiled live at CES

  * Bipedal robot for complex and unstructured environments
  * Uses deep learning AI for advanced, human like adaptability in tasks
* Hyundai’s automation roadmap:

  * Aim to automate up to 40 percent of vehicle assembly
  * Target production cost reduction of roughly 30 percent
  * Target build time reduction of roughly 40 percent
* Atlas pilots are running at a Georgia factory to demonstrate real world capabilities in advance of broader deployment
* Other robotic platforms:

  * Quadruped robot Spot
  * Logistics robot Stretch
* Vehicles and mobility:

  * Hyundai, Kia, and Genesis continue to emphasize software defined vehicles, EV concepts, and potentially urban air mobility via the Supernal eVTOL unit

### 3.3 Geely Auto

* First time Media Days participant at CES
* Uses CES to introduce its latest electric vehicles and smart, connected car platforms to international audiences
* Geely’s global portfolio includes brands such as Volvo, Polestar, and Lotus
* Focus: smart EVs, advanced driver assistance, and autonomous demonstrations

### 3.4 Major Auto OEMs and Suppliers

Traditional global automakers and suppliers maintain a strong presence at CES 2026.

* OEMs: General Motors, BMW, Mercedes Benz, Toyota, Stellantis, and others

  * Use CES to show concept vehicles, EV platforms, and autonomous technologies
* Key supplier highlights:

  * LG Innotek: world leading automotive lighting innovations, likely adaptive headlamps and advanced LED systems
  * Mobileye and similar companies: improved driver assistance and autonomous vision systems
  * AUTOCrypt: next generation vehicle cybersecurity solutions for connected cars
  * STRADVISION with AMD: AI based perception for autonomous driving

The broader theme is that vehicle intelligence depends on both advanced silicon and sophisticated software.

### 3.5 Advanced Mobility

The Vehicle Tech and Advanced Mobility area spans new modes of transportation.

* Micro mobility: e bikes, scooters, delivery robots
* Air taxis and eVTOL concepts from players that have exhibited in the past
* Notable Innovation Award honorees:

  * Strutt “ev¹” vehicle

    * CES 2026 Innovation Award honoree
    * Previewed during a dedicated “Strutt Day” event ahead of CES
  * Oshkosh Striker Volterra Electric Fire Truck

    * Best of Innovation in Vehicle Tech and Advanced Mobility
    * Airport rescue firefighting EV
  * Oshkosh robotic boom lift

    * Innovation Award in Robotics

These examples reflect a trend toward electrification and automation in heavy duty and commercial vehicles, closely tied to sustainability goals.

### 3.6 Summary of Mobility Themes

Attendees see:

* Extensive EV portfolios from global automakers
* Smarter cockpits with high end, award winning displays
* Autonomous driving and advanced driver assistance systems
* Robots building vehicles behind the scenes

Transportation tech is firmly positioned as one of the focal points of CES 2026, with sustainability and autonomy at the center.

## 4. Consumer Electronics and Smart Home

CES 2026 continues to be the primary stage for cutting edge consumer electronics, from giant TVs to smart appliances and connected home systems.

### 4.1 Next Generation Televisions

TV brands use CES as a competitive showcase for new display technologies.

* LG introduces premium LCD TVs using Micro RGB (RGB LED) backlighting

  * Very small red, green, and blue LEDs
  * Improved color gamut and energy efficiency compared to miniLED
  * Targets brighter and more color accurate 8K displays
* LG Signature OLED T

  * Transparent OLED TV
  * Recognized as a Best of Innovation winner
  * Reinforces ongoing focus on transparent and rollable OLED displays
* Samsung:

  * Presents new MicroLED and QD OLED TVs
  * Larger sizes and more accessible models
  * Industry chatter mentions 98 inch 8K MicroLED prototypes and improved quantum dot OLED panels
* Cross brand trends:

  * AI powered image processing and upscaling
  * 8K resolution
  * Better HDR performance for movies, gaming, and streaming

### 4.2 Audio and Home Entertainment

The audio and media hardware category includes:

* Dolby Atmos enabled soundbars and home theater systems
* Wireless and multi room audio solutions
* High resolution audio players for enthusiasts
* StreamUnlimited’s customizable voice AI integration for audio products

  * Suggests new classes of smart speakers and hi fi systems with embedded large language models and richer conversational control

Gaming monitors and streaming gadgets bridge entertainment and gaming, with rumors and speculation about devices such as a possible new gaming console or VR hardware from established PC gaming companies.

### 4.3 Smart Home Devices

The smart home story centers on AI and interoperability.

* Matter standard adoption across:

  * Smart thermostats
  * Security cameras
  * Video doorbells
  * Smart lighting
* Energy efficiency and home health are core themes:

  * DESLOC Self Sustained Smart Lock

    * Uses perovskite solar panels on the lock itself
    * Example of battery free, ambient energy powered smart hardware
  * “Rebreeze” smart window mounted air purifier

    * Reflects integrated, health focused home solutions
* AI enhanced home security and monitoring:

  * Cameras that identify pets vs intruders
  * DIY smart robots that patrol or manage basic tasks

### 4.4 Appliances and Kitchen Tech

Large appliance makers debut:

* AI powered ovens that recognize food and automatically select cooking programs
* Refrigerators with internal cameras for inventory and nutrition tracking
* Washers and dryers that optimize resource usage
* Bathroom tech connected to broader home health dashboards

Anker’s SOLIX C1000 Gen 2 home energy storage unit stands out in the Home Appliances category, illustrating the blending of appliances with energy storage and management.

### 4.5 Major Consumer Electronics Brands

Key brands:

* Samsung: TVs, monitors, Galaxy tablets and laptops, wearables, SmartThings ecosystem
* LG: TVs, appliances, smart home systems
* Sony: Gaming (including PlayStation VR2 related content), cameras and image sensors, premium audio and video gear

## 7. Digital Health and Wellness

Digital health is now a core CES pillar, backed by a dedicated Digital Health Summit spanning the show.

### 7.1 Wearables and Health Monitoring

* Smartwatches, fitness bands, and smart rings with advanced biometrics
* Ear worn devices like Earflo that measure health metrics or hearing performance
* Electronic skin platforms for skincare and drug delivery
* Smart rings as a new major category alongside watches and bands

Sensors are smaller and more precise, with AI performing deeper analysis on sleep, stress, and cardiovascular signals.

### 7.2 Telehealth and Remote Care

* Telemedicine platforms that connect patients and clinicians virtually
* AI assistants that triage symptoms and monitor chronic conditions at home
* Remote patient monitoring kits for hospital at home models

These solutions shift more care from hospitals to homes while maintaining clinical oversight.

### 7.3 Healthcare AI and Diagnostics

At CES 2026, attendees see:

* AI tools reading scans and images faster and with strong accuracy
* Wearable devices detecting anomalies in heart rhythms and other biosignals
* Assistive devices such as smart glasses for blind users, hearing aids that double as consumer earbuds, and accessibility tools powered by AI vision and language

Accessibility and inclusivity are hallmarks of many digital health Innovation Award winners.

### 7.4 Fitness, Wellness, and Longevity

* AI guided workout equipment and smart mats
* VR and AR fitness experiences
* Recovery technologies such as percussion massagers and heat or light therapy devices
* Sports tech such as smart insoles that analyze running form and help prevent injury

Longevity tech and personalized wellness are emerging as strong subthemes.

### 7.5 Medical Devices and Big Health Exhibitors

Large medical and health technology companies bring:

* Continuous glucose monitoring systems
* Connected stethoscopes and imaging tools
* Dental care devices that use AI for better tracking and guidance

Fintech and healthcare intersect in solutions that help patients and providers manage payments and benefits.
